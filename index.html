About Me

Dr. Yang is currently a Humboldt Research Fellow at TU Bergakademie Freiberg, Germany, hosted by Prof. Heinz Konietzky. He received his PhD from Central South University under the supervision of Prof. Ming Tao. During his doctoral studies, he was a joint PhD researcher at the University of Melbourne, working with Prof. Guillermo A. Narsilio. In addition, he served as a guest researcher at Monash University, collaborating with Prof. P.G. Ranjith. He has published more than 50 papers in top international journals such as International Journal of Rock Mechanics and Mining Sciences, Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering, Engineering Geology, Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering, Computers and Geotechnics, with 5 papers selected as ESI highly cited papers (top 1%). His Google Scholar citations have reached 800+, with an H-index of 20. He once received Fundamental Research Funds for the Central Universities of China. He has applied for more than 30 Chinese invention patents. He is the Associate Editor for Frontiers in Earth Science and a Guest Editor for Applied Sciences. He also serves as a reviewer for well-known journals including International Journal of Rock Mechanics and Mining Sciences, Journal of Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Engineering, Tunnelling and Underground Space Technology, International Journal For Numerical Methods in Engineering, and Computers and Geotechnics.

He integrated X-ray diffraction, digital image processing, Newton's Second Law, and Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction to establish a multi-scale, multi-field modeling framework that reproduces the characteristics of real rock masses, aiming to provide more effective solutions for clean energy development. His research interests include deep rock fragmentation, underground energy storage, thermal–hydraulic–mechanical–chemical (THMC) coupling in rock engineering, the development of discrete element method/finite-discrete element method (DEM/FDEM) numerical models, and engineering applications.
